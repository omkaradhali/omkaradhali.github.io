I'm a software engineer at [Memorial Sloan Kettering Cancer Center](https://www.mskcc.org/), where I work on the systems that support clinical genomics reporting — the software that helps pathologists sign out cancer diagnoses.

My day job sits at the intersection of bioinformatics, healthcare infrastructure, and engineering leadership. Over the past six years I've worked across NGS reporting pipelines, HL7 integrations, EHR interoperability, and cloud migrations. Most recently I led a cross-functional team through a hospital-wide EPIC migration — the kind of project where correctness isn't optional.

Outside of work I run, read, and occasionally write things down.

I read a lot of fiction — literary novels, science fiction, and a healthy amount of LitRPG. I run because it's the one hour of the day where I'm not in front of a screen.

This site is where the writing lives. Three kinds:

- **Technical** — engineering, AI/ML, things I figured out the hard way
- **Creative** — essays, experiments, things that don't fit anywhere else
- **Life lessons** — observations I keep returning to

If something here resonates, I'm on [GitHub](https://github.com/omkaradhali).
